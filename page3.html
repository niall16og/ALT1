Project Ideas


  1. The first idea we had was creating the electronics in a bathroom, such as motion activated lights and an extraction fan.

  2. From the first idea, we developed another idea of making general room electronics including motion activated lights and an automatic fan based on temperature with a temperature display. All with an aim at those that may be less able to control these electronics through more manual means.


Chosen Project


The project we have chosen to do is the 2nd idea we had. We chose this project because we felt it helped contribute to the resolving of accessibility issues in home environments. The motion sensor means that no physical input is required from a user after entering the room, while a thermistor ensures the fan is only on when the temperature is higher than desired, reducing distractions and saving energy.


Design and Function


This project uses two circuits to deliver the desired outputs, one circuit for detecting motion and another for reading temperature and giving outputs. When the first circuit detects motion it will send a radio message “on” to the second circuit which will then turn on an LED light. When the first circuit stops detecting human presence it sends another radio message “off“ to the second circuit, which will then turn off the light. While the second circuit is running the thermistor will always check if the temperature is above the desired level, in this case 20°C, if it is, the motor turns on until the temperature is back to or below the desired temperature. There is also a screen on the second circuit that displays the temperature in the room.

The Circuits

Circuit 1


This circuit will be used to detect motion, sending radio signals to the other circuit, telling it to either turn on or off the light.

Circuit 2


This circuit will detect and display the temperature, and control the fan based the current temperature. It also will turn on and off the light when instructed to do so by the first circuit.
